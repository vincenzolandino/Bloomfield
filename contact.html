Why manufacturers choose Bloomfield

Quoting is the lifeblood of a job shop, and for most manufacturers it depends entirely on one or two estimators who carry decades of pricing knowledge in their heads. When those estimators are out sick, overloaded, or approaching retirement, the shop either slows down or starts guessing. Manufacturers that respond to RFQs in under two days win bids at nearly three times the rate of shops that take five or more days. Speed matters, and speed requires access to historical job data, material costs, and customer-specific context that no spreadsheet can surface fast enough.

The knowledge gap compounds over time. Every experienced machinist, engineer, and production manager carries institutional knowledge that never gets documented. Setup notes live in personal notebooks. Process adjustments get passed down verbally between shifts. When someone retires or leaves, that knowledge disappears overnight. Bloomfield builds knowledge capture systems that pull from job records, process specs, machine history, and operator notes to make that expertise searchable and permanent.

Disconnected systems make visibility nearly impossible. ERP holds the job data. A whiteboard tracks what is running on the floor. A shared drive has the drawings. Email has the customer communications. Nobody sees the full picture at the same time. By the time a late shipment gets flagged, the problem started two weeks earlier in a scheduling decision nobody had the data to make differently. Bloomfield connects those data sources into a single intelligence layer your team can query in plain English.

We work exclusively with manufacturers because the problems are specific and the stakes are real. Off-the-shelf AI tools built for generic business use cannot understand the difference between a 5-axis aerospace part and a hydraulic manifold. Custom software built around your operation, your data, and your team's language is what makes AI useful on a shop floor.
